The ISO/TS 15066 biomechanical limits builder produces a structured xlsx workbook that maps measured contact force and pressure values against the threshold limits defined in ISO/TS 15066:2016 Table A.2 for every relevant body region. It classifies each contact event as quasi-static (clamping) or transient, records a pass/fail determination per region, and forms the foundational safety evidence file that all downstream cobot lane skills — SSM plan, PFL plan, and hand-guiding spec — consume as their authoritative biomechanical input.

## Standard reference

**ISO/TS 15066:2016** — *Robots and robotic devices — Collaborative robots*

Section 5.4 (Power and Force Limiting) and Annex A (Table A.2) define the biomechanical basis for all cobot safety modes. Table A.2 specifies allowable force and pressure limits for 29 body regions under two contact type regimes.

<Info>
  ISO/TS 15066 is a Technical Specification, not a full International Standard. It is referenced normatively by ISO 10218-2:2025 for collaborative operation requirements. Compliance with Table A.2 values is the primary means of demonstrating that PFL safeguarding meets the safety requirements of the standard.
</Info>

## Contact type classification rules

| Contact type | Definition | Key threshold distinction |
|---|---|---|
| Transient | Short-duration impact; robot and human surfaces separate immediately after contact | Higher allowable force; duration < 0.5 s |
| Quasi-static | Clamping contact; human body part trapped between robot and fixed structure | Lower allowable force; applies full clamping load |

<Warning>
  When workspace geometry allows a human body part to be clamped between the robot and a fixed surface (fixture, table edge, frame), the quasi-static threshold — which is more restrictive — **must** be used for that body region regardless of intended contact duration.
</Warning>

## Instrument setup guidance

```text
Recommended instrumentation stack:
1. Biofidelic force measurement device (e.g., Pilz Prism or equivalent)
2. Calibrated force/pressure sensor matched to body region geometry
3. Data logger at ≥ 1 kHz sample rate
4. Robot programmed to execute worst-case speed and approach angle
5. Three repeat measurements per region; use maximum recorded value
```

<Tip>
  Run measurements at the maximum programmed speed and the minimum configured separation distance to capture the true worst-case contact energy. Values recorded at reduced test speeds do not satisfy the measurement methodology requirement.
</Tip>
