The Power and Force Limiting plan builder produces a structured xlsx workbook that documents how a cobot application complies with the PFL safeguarding mode defined in ISO/TS 15066:2016. PFL relies on the robot's inherent ability to limit the forces applied during contact to levels that are biomechanically safe, making physical contact permissible without triggering an emergency stop. The builder ingests the upstream biomechanical limits workbook, adds biofidelic measurement records and test setup documentation, and classifies each contact scenario by type to determine the applicable threshold.

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Transient contact" icon="bolt">
        Short-duration impact where the robot and human surfaces separate immediately. Applicable to hand, arm, and shoulder contacts in free-space robot motions. Higher allowable force threshold.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Quasi-static clamping contact" icon="lock">
        Human body part becomes trapped between the robot (or its payload) and a fixed structure. Lower allowable force threshold applies. Must be assessed wherever clamping geometry exists.

## Standard reference

**ISO/TS 15066:2016, Section 5.4** — *Power and Force Limiting (PFL)*

PFL is the most commonly applied safeguarding mode for physical human–robot collaboration. The standard permits contact to occur, relying on the robot's force/torque sensing or inherent compliance to keep contact forces below the biomechanical thresholds in Table A.2. PFL requires both force limiting and power limiting to be active simultaneously.

<Info>
  ISO/TS 15066 does not specify how the robot implements PFL — it may be via joint torque sensing, admittance control, or direct force sensing at the tool. The measurement requirement is for the output contact force, regardless of the implementation method.
</Info>

## Measurement instrumentation

Biofidelic force measurement devices simulate the mechanical properties of human tissue to measure the effective contact force experienced by the body region, rather than the raw actuator force. Generic load cells do not satisfy the biofidelic requirement.

| Instrument type | Applicability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Biofidelic measurement device (e.g., Pilz Prism, ATI) | All body regions | Required for ISO/TS 15066 compliance measurements |
| ISO/TS 15066 compliant dummy | Quasi-static clamping scenarios | Simulates trapped-limb geometry |
| Robot internal torque sensing | Power limit verification only | Not sufficient for contact force measurement |

<Tip>
  Document the biofidelic device model, firmware version, and calibration certificate number in the test setup tab. Auditors will request calibration traceability; missing records are one of the most common PFL review findings.
</Tip>
