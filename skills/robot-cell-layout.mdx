The robot cell layout builder produces a structured xlsx workbook that defines the complete physical footprint of an industrial robot cell, including perimeter fence design, light curtain placement, safeguarded space boundaries, work zone delineation (teaching, maintenance, and automatic), aisle clearances, emergency egress paths, and safety sign locations. Every dimension is validated against the safety distance calculation methods defined in ISO 13857, and the layout feeds directly into the safety I/O matrix and EOAT specification that follow it in the cell design lane.

        Three-zone delineation table: automatic operation zone (robot energized, no human access), teaching zone (reduced speed, enabling device required), and maintenance zone (LOTO applied, full access). Includes permitted activities and required PPE per zone.

      <Step title="Light Curtain Placement">
        For each light curtain or area scanner: device location coordinates, detection field dimensions, minimum object sensitivity, and the ISO 13857 safety distance calculation showing the required mounting standoff from the nearest hazard.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Emergency Egress">
        Egress path widths, door swing directions, maximum travel distance to nearest exit, and signage schedule per OSHA 1910.37 minimum clearance requirements.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Safety Sign Schedule">
        Sign type, legend text, ISO 7010 pictogram reference, mounting location (coordinate reference), and mounting height for every required sign in the cell.
      </Step>

## Standards reference

**ISO 10218-2:2011 (revised 2025)** — *Robots and robotic devices — Safety requirements for industrial robots — Part 2: Robot systems and integration*

Clause 5.2 (safeguarding) and Clause 5.4 (workspace design) define the cell layout requirements that this skill's output addresses.

**ISO 13857:2019** — *Safety of machinery — Safety distances to prevent hazard zones being reached by upper and lower limbs*

<Info>
  ISO 13857 provides the safety distance calculation methods used in the light curtain placement tab. Annex A covers reaching over low protective structures; Annex B covers reaching through openings. Both are referenced in the safety distance compliance reviewer check.
</Info>

## Safety distance formula

```text
Ds = K × (Ts + Tc + Tr + Tbm)

Where:
  Ds  = minimum safety distance (mm)
  K   = approach speed of the body or body part (mm/s), typically 1600 mm/s for hand/arm
  Ts  = stopping time of the machine (s)
  Tc  = response time of the control system (s)
  Tr  = response time of the safeguard (s)
  Tbm = additional time for brake monitor (s), if applicable
```

<Warning>
  The approach speed constant K = 1600 mm/s applies only when the safeguard cannot detect the presence of a person who has moved past it. If the safeguard field is a point or line (e.g., a single-beam light curtain), use the appropriate body approach speed from ISO 13857 Table B.1 rather than the default hand approach speed.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  Request an overhead photograph or facility drawing from the integrator before running this skill. Accurate column locations, utility drops, and existing aisle markings prevent layout revisions that propagate downstream into the safety I/O matrix and EOAT specification.
</Tip>
